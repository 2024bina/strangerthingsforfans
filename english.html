Meet Stranger Things
synopsis

When a boy disappears, the whole town participates in the search. But what they find are secrets, supernatural forces and a girl.

Set in the fictional town of Hawkins, Indiana, during the 1980s, the plot of the first season takes place in 1983 and focuses on the disappearance of student Will Byers, and presents the journey of his friends to find him, until on the way, they encounter a strange girl with telekinetic powers, who escaped from a laboratory that used children as guinea pigs for supernatural experiments. The second season, titled Stranger Things 2, takes place a year later, and deals with the characters' attempts to return to normality and the consequences caused by the events of the first season, but Will gets sequels from the inverted world. The third season, titled Stranger Things 3, takes place in the American summer of 1985, where the characters must deal with the onset of adolescence and new supernatural events after the opening of a shopping mall in the city and the arrival of a dangerous Russian team that plans to open the portal of the inverted world. Season 4 is scheduled to arrive in 2022



process of creating the series

The American streaming television series Stranger things of the genres science fiction, horror, suspense and teen drama, created, written and directed by brothers Matt and Ross Duffer for the Netflix platform. The Duffer brothers, Shawn Levy and Dan Cohen are also the executive producers.

The Duffer brothers developed the premise of the series in mid-2015, and in the original script, the project would be called Montauk, and would be set in the city of the same name located in New York, addressing conspiracy theories involving the u.S. government's secret experiments at a military base in the city in the early 1980s. After defining the script, the Duffer brothers included several references to the cultural elements of that decade, such as science fiction films, supernatural horror and action and adventure, as well as video games, anime and music, as well as numerous references to works by Steven Spielberg, John Carpenter and Stephen King, considered the duffer brothers' great inspirations for the project. They were also inspired by strange experiments that took place during the Cold War and real-world conspiracy theories involving secret government experiments.



Cast

The series premiered worldwide on July 15, 2016 and features in its cast the names of Winona Ryder, David Harbour, Finn Wolfhard, Millie Bobby Brown, Gaten Matarazzo, Caleb McLaughlin, Noah Schnapp, Natalia Dyer, Charlie Heaton, Joe Keery, Cara Buono and Matthew Modine, while Sadie Sink, Dacre Montgomery, Sean Astin, Paul Reiser, Maya Hawke, Priah Ferguson and Brett Gelman were included in the cast in later seasons
